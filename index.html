IsoTex: Creating Isotypes as Documents with Semi-Declarative Markup Language

Isotypes are an omnipresent form of visualization favored by designers to communicate data to a large audience. Crafting these representations requires a designer to compose a palette of icons and define units of data encoded by each icon. The choice of palette and unit may result in vastly different isotypes, varying in visual output, and possibly conveying contradictory insights about the same data. Thus, exploring the design space of isotypes is a critical part of the creation process. This paper contributes IsoTex, a new semi-declarative markup language for creating isotypes. This syntax works akin to document preparation and markup languages, separating unit specification from architectural syntax. Coupled with an interactive editor, IsoTex enable designers to explore a large range of design alternatives via live editing or direct manipulation. For example, users can experiment with different data units and layouts in the language, immediately reflected in the editor, or rapidly skim through alternative icons, adjusting their aspect ratio and spacing in the editor, immediately added to the syntax. The light-weight version history supports design experimentations while the textual specification facilitates design reuse. We demonstrate the range of isotypes that can be created using this expressive language in a collection of examples. Feedback from users suggests that IsoTex facilitates design explorations compare to graphical editors while offering a lower learning curve than programming.
